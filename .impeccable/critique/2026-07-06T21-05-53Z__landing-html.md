## Overall Impression

The page's technical bones are more disciplined than its "feels generic/AI-made" reputation suggests — the accent-scarcity discipline, the bespoke torch-hero, real screenshots, and correct alt text are not things a template spits out. The generic feeling comes from two concrete, fixable culprits: the eyebrow/kicker label device (present, though less pervasive than DESIGN.md's own note implied) and the tilted-shadow "photo-pinned" treatment on exactly the two most important images on the page. Fix those two, tighten the roadmap section (both its 5-item chunking violation and its placement right before the closing CTA), and add a persistent nav CTA, and this stops reading as templated. The single biggest opportunity: the emotional close is currently a list of unbuilt features right before the ask — reordering so the local-first "it's just files" pitch is the last thing a visitor reads before the CTA would turn a soft landing into a strong one.

## What's Working

1. **The torch-hero mechanic is real craft.** `TorchHero.tsx` ties the cursor-as-light-source directly to the product's actual fog-of-war feature, defaults sensibly for non-pointer users, and fully no-ops under `prefers-reduced-motion` via an early `matchMedia` check before ever attaching a listener — content stays visible regardless, exactly per the WCAG AA bar in PRODUCT.md.
2. **Copy specificity in the hero subhead.** "parede bloqueia visão, não é só um círculo apagado" persuades through technical specificity, not adjectives — "show, don't tell" working in prose form.
3. **Honest imagery.** Real app screenshots with genuinely descriptive alt text throughout (not filler) — something templated pages almost never get right.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] `--text-faint` token fails WCAG AA contrast everywhere it's used**
**Why it matters:** `#7c735e` on `#14110d` (ink) computes to 4.01:1, on `#1c1811` (ink-2) to 3.76:1, on `#221d15` (ink-3) to 3.56:1 — all below the 4.5:1 AA floor for normal text, and none of these uses (hero-note 0.82rem, section-footnote 0.86rem, figcaption 0.76rem) are large enough for the 3:1 large-text exception. This isn't a one-off: it hits `.hero-note` (the free/open-source/MIT-license line — arguably the single most important trust signal on the page for a skeptical first-timer), `.section-footnote`, every screenshot `figcaption`, and the entire footer.
**Fix:** Lighten `--text-faint` to roughly `#8f8570` or darken the ink steps it sits on, then re-verify against all three backgrounds.
**Suggested command:** /impeccable audit

**[P1] No persistent CTA in the sticky nav**
**Why it matters:** The header has brand + 3 anchor links + a GitHub text link but no download button, despite DESIGN.md's own component spec assuming one exists. Once a visitor scrolls past the hero, the only path back to "Baixar Jaguar" is scrolling to the very bottom — a direct conversion leak on a page whose entire job is converting scroll into download.
**Fix:** Add the ember `.btn` to the nav.
**Suggested command:** /impeccable layout

**[P1] The eyebrow/kicker label device and the photo-pinned shadow+rotation treatment are the two concrete drivers of the "feels AI-made" impression**
**Why it matters:** This is the user's own stated reaction to the page, and both assessments converge on naming these as the actual causes rather than any of the louder, more commonly-cited AI-slop tells (which are absent here). `.eyebrow`/`.section-kicker` are visually near-identical devices under two different class names; the shadow+rotation motif lands on exactly the two most prominent images (hero screenshot, ledger card), which is a recognizable Webflow/Framer template signature.
**Fix:** Replace the eyebrow/kicker with a genuinely different section-opening device (per DESIGN.md's own Don'ts), and decide deliberately whether the photo-pinned treatment stays anywhere at all — don't let it persist by default on the two highest-visibility images.
**Suggested command:** /impeccable quieter

**[P2] Roadmap section breaks the page's own chunking discipline and sits in the worst possible spot**
**Why it matters:** Both legend blocks correctly cap at 4 items; the roadmap has 5, the only exception on an otherwise disciplined page. Worse, it's the section immediately before the closing CTA — meaning the last substantive content before the "download now" ask is a list of features that don't exist yet (cloud sync, marketplace, token creator, handouts, GM notes), which is a weak peak-end note for a conversion page. Compounding this, every roadmap item hardcodes the same `"planejado"` (planned) status tag regardless of actual proximity to shipping.
**Fix:** Cut to 4 items or split into two groups; move the roadmap earlier (context-setting) and let the local-first "it's just files" pitch be the last thing read before the CTA; make status per-item or drop the pretense of variability.
**Suggested command:** /impeccable layout

**[P2] No Open Graph / social preview metadata**
**Why it matters:** `landing.html`'s head has only description, title, favicon, and font preconnects — no `og:title`, `og:image`, `og:description`, or Twitter card tags. This audience shares links into Discord servers and r/rpg-style communities, exactly where an unstyled link preview costs distribution.
**Fix:** Add OG tags pointing at one of the existing screenshots.
**Suggested command:** /impeccable harden

**[P3] Hero screenshot shows full app chrome instead of the persuasive content**
**Why it matters:** `hero.png` gives roughly a third of the frame to the asset sidebar and toolbar, including visible placeholder hint text ("Selecione uma ferramenta na barra lateral") — none of which is the actual persuasive content (painted map + colored light), on the single highest-visibility image on the page.
